Privacy Policy

Last updated: 5 July 2026 · Published by Sloom Software

The short version. The Sloom Studio app is local-first with no user accounts and no Sloom servers: your projects, media, and API keys stay on your device, and the app sends nothing about you to us. The only content that leaves your device is what you choose to send to a third-party AI provider with your own credentials. The one exception is this website, which keeps privacy-respecting, cookieless visit counts and offers an optional email signup you control. Details below.

1. Information we collect

Through the Sloom Studio app, we collect no personal data. The app contains no analytics, advertising SDKs, tracking, or crash-reporting that sends data to us, and it talks to no Sloom backend. (For the sloom.studio website, see section 1a.)

1a. This website (sloom.studio)

Analytics. This marketing site uses first-party, cookieless visit measurement: it records aggregate page views and the referring website’s host name (for example “google.com”). There are no cookies, no cross-site tracking, no advertising networks, and no third-party analytics services. We never place your data in a URL, and we do not build profiles of visitors.

Email signup (optional). If you choose to enter your email in the signup form, we store that address on our own server for one purpose: to email you occasional Sloom Studio updates. We do not sell or share it, and you can unsubscribe at any time by replying to any email or writing support@sloom.studio. If you never submit the form, we never have your email.

2. Data stored on your device

Sloom Studio stores the following locally on your device so the app can work. This data is not transmitted to us:

  • Provider API keys and settings you enter, kept in local app storage so you don’t re-enter them.
  • Projects (.sloom files) and the media you import or generate.
  • App preferences (layout, tool, and UI settings).

You can remove this data at any time by deleting your projects, clearing the app’s storage, or uninstalling the app.

3. Data you send to third-party AI providers

Sloom Studio is a bring-your-own-key tool. When you run a generation, edit, or other AI action, the app sends the inputs needed for that action — for example your prompt text, and any image, video, or audio you selected — directly from your device to the AI provider you chose for that action, authenticated with the API key you supplied. Depending on your configuration this may include Google (Gemini / Vertex AI), OpenAI or OpenAI-compatible endpoints, Hugging Face, Stability AI, Black Forest Labs, Atlas Cloud, ElevenLabs, and similar AI providers you configure.

These providers are independent companies. Your use of them is governed by their terms and privacy policies, and any usage is billed to you by them. We are not a party to that relationship and never receive a copy of that content. Please review each provider’s privacy policy before sending content to it. These transfers happen only as a result of an action you initiate and only to the destination you selected. Sloom Studio does not send your content anywhere on its own.

4. Permissions

  • Internet — to communicate with the AI providers you configure.
  • Storage (legacy, older Android only) — to save your projects and exported files to a shared folder. On modern Android (13+) no storage permission is requested at all.

When you import media, you choose the file through the standard Android file picker, which grants Sloom Studio access only to the file you selected — no broad photo/media permission is requested. All media is processed on your device.

5. Children’s privacy

Sloom Studio is a professional creative tool intended for general/older audiences. It is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect any information from children.

6. Data security

Because your data stays on your device and we hold none of it, there is no Sloom-side database to breach. Network requests to AI providers are made over encrypted (HTTPS) connections. Protecting the device itself (lock screen, OS updates, who has access) is your responsibility.

7. Data deletion

You are in control of all data. To delete it: delete individual .sloom projects, clear Sloom Studio’s app storage in Android Settings, and/or uninstall the app. To delete content you previously sent to an AI provider, use that provider’s own controls.

8. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy we will update the “Last updated” date above and post the new version at this URL.

9. Contact

Questions about this policy: support@sloom.studio.
